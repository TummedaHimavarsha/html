Baahubali is an Indian epic action film directed by S. S. Rajamouli, released in two parts: Baahubali: The Beginning (2015) and Baahubali: The Conclusion (2017). The movie tells the story of Amarendra Baahubali and his son Mahendra Baahubali, set in the fictional kingdom of Mahishmati. The film is praised for its grand visuals, powerful storytelling, and breathtaking battle sequences. It showcases themes of bravery, sacrifice, betrayal, and justice. The performances of Prabhas, Rana Daggubati, Anushka Shetty, and Tamannaah were widely acclaimed. About Director

S. S. Rajamouli (born 10 October 1973) is one of India’s most successful and visionary film directors. Known for his grand storytelling, innovative ideas, and spectacular visuals, he has redefined Indian cinema on a global scale. Rajamouli gained fame through movies like Magadheera, Eega, Baahubali: The Beginning, Baahubali: The Conclusion, and the Oscar-winning RRR. His films are celebrated for their emotional depth, powerful characters, and technical brilliance. Rajamouli is admired for blending Indian culture with universal storytelling, making his movies appeal to audiences worldwide. Today, he is regarded as one of the greatest filmmakers in Indian cinema history.

About music director

M. M. Keeravani (born July 4, 1961) is a highly acclaimed Indian music composer, singer, and lyricist, known for his work in Telugu, Hindi, and other South Indian films. He gained international fame for composing the song “Naatu Naatu” from the film RRR, for which he won an Oscar and a Golden Globe Award. With a career spanning over three decades, Keeravani is celebrated for his musical versatility and has earned numerous awards, including multiple National Film Awards, Nandi Awards, and Filmfare Awards.
